Ernakulam

Ernakulam, the urbane face of Kochi, is a bustling place with crowded shopping malls, restaurants and high-rises. On the cultural, political, commercial and industrial fronts, Ernakulam is on the cutting edge, as far as the State is concerned.Fort Kochi has a quaint ambience and the colonial buildings, telling the tales of centuries-old cultural and commercial interaction with the colonial powers of the West, makes a big impression. Places to visit:
Fort Kochi

Fort Kochi, the western part of the Kochi city of Ernakulam district in Kerala.Fort Kochi has played an important role in the history of Kerala. Fort Kochi also has several attractions like the Santa Cruz Basilica.

Cherai Beach

Cherai Beach is near to Vypeen Island in Ernakulam district of Kerala. It is an ideal spot for swimming and viewing the Chinese fishing nets or Cheena Vala.

Bolgatty Palace

Bolgatty Island is a picturesque island located near Fort Kochi in Ernakulam district. It is famous for its eponymous palace along with being a growing cosmopolitan hotspot in the area.

Hill Palace Mueseum

Hill Palace is an archaeological museum and palace located in the Tripunithura neighbourhood of Kochi city in the Indian state of Kerala. It is the largest archeological museum in the state and was the imperial administrative office and official residence of the Cochin Maharaja.
